§ 03 · The Business, Plainly

We turn abandoned websites into durable digital property.

When a small software company closes down, its web address (domain) is often the most valuable asset it leaves behind. Those domains go unclaimed for 30–90 days, then drop into a public pool where most are snatched by automated bots that resell them for pocket change. We identify the ones that are actually valuable, acquire them, and put them back to productive use.

The simple version

Think of it as buying the phone number from a closed hardware store. The number still rings with customer calls. Those calls have value — we either route them to a competing store (which pays us for leads), or we park the line with generic information and earn advertising revenue.

Web domains work the same way, but for internet traffic. A well-known SaaS company's domain pulls thousands of visitors a month from search engines and old links for years after the company is gone.

Why it's a business, not a hobby

The process requires a specific cross-disciplinary skillset: reading federal bankruptcy filings, understanding domain authority metrics, and navigating registrar auction mechanics. Very few people have all three.

We built software that automates the entire pipeline end-to-end. Once deployed, it identifies roughly 5 to 15 qualified acquisition targets per month with zero ongoing human effort.

§ 04 · Operations

How the pipeline runs.

The full cycle runs as an automated process nightly. Each stage below expands to show what happens, backed by production code already written. Click any stage for detail.

Runtime. The whole pipeline completes in about three hours, nightly, on a single $32/month rented server. No employees required.
01
Identify candidates from public federal records
Pull yesterday's Chapter 7 and 11 filings with tech keywords. Typical yield: 5–20 candidates per day.

PACER (Public Access to Court Electronic Records) is the federal government's public database of every bankruptcy filing. The system pulls the previous day's tech-related filings and cross-references with a secondary free archive for completeness.

expand
02
Resolve company names to web domains
Industry-standard name-to-domain lookup services identify each company's website.

Court filings list legal names, not websites. A cascade of commercial lookup services resolves about 70% of candidates. The rest are filtered out.

expand
03
Score value using objective SEO metrics
Composite score from Ahrefs domain authority + AI topical relevance. Threshold: 60 out of 100.

Each domain gets scored using Ahrefs (the industry-standard SEO authority metric) combined with topical relevance. Domains below 60 are dropped before any acquisition spend.

expand
04
Acquire the domain when available
Place backorders across three drop-catch registrars simultaneously. Historical success rate: 60–80%.

When a domain's registration expires, there's a ~30-day public release window. We use three specialized services in parallel — paying only for successful acquisitions (typically $60–80 per catch).

expand
05
Monetize via the appropriate revenue path
Higher-authority domains redirect paying traffic; lower-authority domains generate ad revenue or list for resale.

Top scorers get pointed at paying partner websites. Mid-tier domains get listed with parking services. Lower-tier domains get listed on established aftermarket platforms for resale.

expand
06
Optional: fractional sale through licensed broker
Premium domains can be wrapped and sold as fractional interests — only through a licensed third party.

A tokenization framework allows fractional ownership sales to accredited investors. We never handle investor funds directly — all settlement flows through Securitize, a licensed broker-dealer.

expand
07
Log every transaction for compliance
Every pipeline run writes to an audit table tagged to the LLC for IRS, state, and municipal reporting.

Every acquisition, every revenue event, every API call writes to a database audit trail. Makes quarterly tax prep straightforward and simplifies Canby business license renewal.

expand
Design principle: do the boring, tedious, compliant work — and let automation make the margins work.
§ 05 · Revenue Model

Three revenue lines. The first two are the backbone.

Path A and Path C are straightforward and well-established — these carry the business. Path B is a premium add-on handled entirely through licensed third parties.

Path A · Primary

Traffic redirect (lead sales)

$300 – $1,000per domain · 7–30 day cycle

Domain's inbound traffic is redirected to a paying partner company's website. Partner pays us for resulting leads on a monthly or quarterly basis. Core cash-flow engine.

Path C · Steady

Domain parking & resale

$5 – $50per domain per month · ongoing

Held domains earn passive advertising revenue through parking services. Lower-value domains are listed on aftermarket platforms (Afternic, Sedo) for outright sale.

Path B · Optional

Fractional sale (via broker)

$1,000 – $10,000+per domain · 6–24 months

Premium domains can be offered as fractional interests through Securitize, which handles all investor-side settlement. We never touch investor funds directly.
